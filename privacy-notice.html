2) What we collect

A) Contact form (Support)

When you send a message via our Support contact form, we collect:

  • Email address
  • Name
  • Subject
  • Your message content

B) Pre‑launch / Trial queue sign‑up

When you join our pre‑launch/trial queue, we collect:

  • Email address (email)
  • Preferred name (preferred_name)
  • Location (free‑text, e.g., country/city) (location)
  • Age (number, 13–99) (age)
  • Occupation (occupation)
  • Product interest (OMGO sphere / OMGO mini / Both) (model_interest)
  • How you heard about us (heard_about)
  • Why you’re interested (optional free‑text) (why_interested)

We also receive technical metadata associated with the submission (for example a form label/type, a submission timestamp, and a page “origin” value). We also use a hidden “honeypot” field to help detect automated spam.

C) Newsletter / email updates

When you subscribe to email updates, we collect:

  • Email address
  • Basic sign‑up metadata (e.g., time and the sign‑up source/page)

D) Anti‑spam and abuse protection (reCAPTCHA)

Our Support contact form and Pre‑launch/Trial sign‑up form use Google reCAPTCHA to protect our forms from spam and abuse. reCAPTCHA may process information about your device and browser to assess whether an interaction is genuine.

Note: Our Newsletter form currently does not use reCAPTCHA.

We use contact form information to respond to your message, provide support, and keep a record of our correspondence.

Pre‑launch / trial queue

We use your trial sign‑up information to:

  • manage the trial queue (e.g., eligibility, prioritisation, and contact scheduling);

  • understand interest and demographics to help us improve the product and plan launch.

Email updates and newsletters

If you opt in to email updates or newsletters, we’ll use your email address to send you OMNX news, OMGO product updates, and (where relevant) early access announcements.

Security and fraud prevention

We use anti‑spam and security measures (including reCAPTCHA, rate‑limiting, and validation checks) to protect our website, forms, and users.

4) Lawful bases

UK GDPR requires us to have a lawful basis for using personal information. The lawful basis can differ depending on the purpose.

A) Support contact form

Legitimate interests — it’s in our legitimate interests to receive and respond to messages from visitors and customers, and to keep records of communications so we can follow up appropriately.

B) Pre‑launch / Trial queue

We process your pre‑launch/trial sign‑up information for two main reasons:

  • Legitimate interests — it’s in our legitimate interests to run the trial queue you asked to join (for example, prioritising and organising participation) and to use high‑level, early‑stage research signals to improve the product.
  • Consent (email contact & updates) — when you tick the required consent box on the trial form, you are asking us to contact you about the OMGO trial queue and pre‑launch updates. Where you tick the optional “email updates” box, you’re also opting in to broader ongoing email updates. You can withdraw your consent at any time.

C) Newsletter / email updates

Consent — we send marketing emails to individuals only when you opt in (unless a narrow “soft opt‑in” exception applies).

D) Security and anti‑spam

Legitimate interests — it’s in our legitimate interests to protect our website and forms from spam, abuse, and fraud.

If you have questions about our lawful bases, contact us and we’ll explain how they apply to your situation.

5) Marketing and preferences

We only send marketing emails to individuals when we have an appropriate basis under PECR — usually your consent.

  • Trial form (required box): if you tick the required consent box on the trial form (consent), you agree to be contacted about the OMGO trial queue and pre‑launch updates.
  • Optional updates: if you tick the optional updates box (email_updates), you’re also opting in to broader ongoing email updates (for example newsletters and future announcements).
  • Unsubscribe any time: every marketing email will include an unsubscribe option, and you can also contact us to opt out.
